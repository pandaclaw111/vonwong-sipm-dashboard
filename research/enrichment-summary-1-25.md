## Key Patterns Across Cases

### What Produces Score 5 Wins
- **Long organizing runway before the tip:** Marriage equality (20+ years), Green Belt Movement (27 years), India Farmers (decades of MSK infrastructure)
- **Clear, specific, measurable demands:** India farmers (repeal three specific laws), Malawi (annul specific election), Liberia (Taylor out, peace deal signed)
- **Institutional pathway existed:** Courts (Malawi, US marriage equality), parliament (India repeal), international tribunal (Taylor conviction)
- **Physical presence over time:** India's 13-month sit-in; Liberia's fish market vigils; Korean weekly escalating candlelight vigils

### What Produces Score 0–1 Failures
- **No institutional consolidation after removing the leader:** Burkina Faso, Mali, Zimbabwe — removed dictator, military fills vacuum
- **State repression capacity exceeds movement's protection:** Morocco Hirak — leaders imprisoned on 20-year sentences
- **Primary demand met but secondary demands hollow:** EndSARS (SARS disbanded, SWAT replaces it), Sudan (Bashir removed, RSF massacres protesters)
- **Movement energy without organizational infrastructure:** Ghana #FixTheCountry — large hashtag, small protest, no follow-through

### Reversals Are Common, Not Exceptions
- **8 of 25 cases** show significant reversals: NoDAPL, Weinstein conviction, BLM police reforms, Chile constitution, Sudan democratic transition, Burkina Faso, Zimbabwe, Mali
- The **most vulnerable window** is 12-24 months after the tip, when energy dissipates and backlash organizes
- **Electoral victories don't automatically translate to movement wins** (NoDAPL under Biden; BLM pledges under corporate pressure)

### The "False Tip" Pattern (Cases 16, 19, 23)
Zimbabwe, Burkina Faso, and Mali all show the same pattern: popular uprising removes leader → military fills vacuum → outcome is comparable or worse than before. The trigger was real; the structural transformation was not.

## Case-Level Highlights

**Case 1 — MeToo:** Speak Out Act and Ending Forced Arbitration Act are lasting legal infrastructure. Time's Up collapsing in 2023 and Weinstein NY conviction overturned in 2024 are underreported setbacks.

**Case 2 — BLM:** $10.6B in corporate pledges largely unfulfilled. BLMGNF financial scandal ($6M misuse allegations) significantly damaged organizational credibility. Most police reform measures reversed within 18 months.

**Case 5 — NoDAPL:** Pipeline still operational. Biden broke explicit promise to stop it. Court challenges continue but oil flows. Keystone XL cancellation is the movement's largest indirect win.

**Case 9 — India Farmers:** Complete win — all three laws literally ceased to exist. But MSP guarantee (the secondary demand) never legislated; 2024 follow-up protest proves the fight continues.

**Case 14 — Sudan:** The 2023 SAF-RSF civil war (April 2023 to present) has produced the world's largest displacement crisis (8M+). The revolution is now net negative by any humanitarian measure.

**Case 23 — Mali:** Scored 0 — the only case in the set where the movement's success directly enabled worse outcomes. Imam Dicko's political irrelevance post-coup is a striking outcome for the movement's most prominent figure.

**Case 25 — Liberia:** The disrobing threat deserves more analytical attention as a case of culturally specific, non-replicable power. Taylor's International Court conviction (first since Nuremberg for a head of state) is the fullest accountability arc in all 25 cases.
