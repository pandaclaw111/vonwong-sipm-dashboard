## 1. AI Labor Displacement Movement

**SIPM Score: 19/25**
- Grievance: 5/5 — Existential threat to entire creative and knowledge-worker classes
- Narrative: 4/5 — "They're stealing our faces, voices, and futures"
- Infrastructure: 4/5 — SAG-AFTRA, WGA, Authors Guild, Teamsters all engaged
- Permission: 3/5 — Public sympathy high but "AI progress" framing muddies waters
- Trigger: 3/5 — 2026 TV/Theatrical negotiations are the next flashpoint

**Status:** Active and escalating. The 2023 Hollywood strikes (SAG-AFTRA + WGA) set landmark precedents for consent and compensation. The 2025 Video Game Strike (SAG-AFTRA vs. game studios) ended with ratified consent/disclosure requirements for AI replicas. The **2026 TV/Theatrical renegotiation is the next major inflection point** — SAG-AFTRA chief negotiator Duncan Crabtree-Ireland has stated they will push for AI training data protections not secured in 2023. Proposed federal NO FAKES Act would create nationwide right of publicity.

**Key protections won so far:**
- Informed consent required for digital replicas of voice/likeness
- Fair compensation for days performer "would have worked"
- Prohibition on using material to train generative AI (in newer commercials contract, 2025)
- California AB 2602 and NY SB 7676B: state-level replica consent laws

**Key Orgs:**
- SAG-AFTRA: https://www.sagaftra.org
- WGA (Writers Guild of America): https://www.wga.org
- Authors Guild: https://www.authorsguild.org
- AI Now Institute (policy research): https://ainowinstitute.org
- Teamsters (beginning to engage on AI/automation): https://teamster.org

**Funders:** Union dues + Open Society Foundations grants to labor/tech policy orgs; Ford Foundation AI ethics funding

**Connections to existing movements:** Labor movement (obviously), democracy (who controls narrative infrastructure), climate (AI voiceovers replacing union travel reduces emissions — but studios capturing savings not workers)

**Benjamin's Opportunity:** 
Visual gap — the **"ghost performer"**: a luminous human figure surrounded by thousands of nearly-identical AI-generated copies of themselves, each labeled with a dollar amount. The original is being erased as their digital twin proliferates infinitely without consent or pay. No one has visualized the scale (billions of replicas) vs. the zero income flowing back. Could be shot as an installation with a single real person surrounded by screens showing AI-generated versions.

**Upcoming trigger:** 2026 SAG-AFTRA TV/Theatrical Agreement negotiations (summer 2026). Also: NO FAKES Act federal vote expected 2026.

## 2. AI Surveillance / Facial Recognition Resistance

**SIPM Score: 18/25**
- Grievance: 5/5 — Wrongful arrests, racial bias, surveillance creep now documented with real victims
- Narrative: 4/5 — "Black faces misidentified, innocent people jailed" — powerful but needs amplification
- Infrastructure: 4/5 — ACLU, EFF, Brennan Center, dozens of city coalitions active
- Permission: 3/5 — Law enforcement lobbying is strong counter-force; "public safety" narrative persists
- Trigger: 2/5 — Milwaukee ban (Feb 2026) and proposed "ICE Out of Our Faces Act" (Feb 2026) are fresh triggers

**Status:** 13+ US cities have banned police facial recognition (SF, Boston, Portland, Oakland, NYC limited). Milwaukee banned it Feb 2026 after public outcry. Clearview AI settled a class-action for $51.75M in March 2025 (class gets 23% equity stake, triggered at IPO). Illinois BIPA remains the gold standard law. EU AI Act includes partial ban on predictive policing systems. New threat vector: Trump administration expanding immigration surveillance — proposed "ICE Out of Our Faces Act" (Feb 2026) would ban ICE/CBP use of biometric ID.

Key concern: Cities that ban facial recognition are outsourcing the work to neighboring jurisdictions — confirmed by Biometric Update reporting (May 2024).

**Funders:** Ford Foundation, Open Society Foundations, MacArthur Foundation; ACLU has large individual donor base

**Connections:** Criminal justice reform, immigration rights, Black Lives Matter infrastructure, indigenous sovereignty (tribal surveillance), climate (smart city surveillance as climate tech)

**Upcoming trigger:** Clearview AI IPO (whenever it happens — class action equity stake kicks in); EU AI Act enforcement deadlines through 2026; No Tech for ICE campaigns intensifying under Trump deportation expansion.

## 3. Algorithmic Bias / Criminal Justice

- Narrative: 3/5 — "COMPAS" and pretrial tools are complex; hard to make visceral
- Infrastructure: 4/5 — Brennan Center, AJL, ACLU, Pretrial Justice Institute all organized
- Permission: 3/5 — "Tech neutrality" myth still powerful in courts
- Trigger: 2/5 — Individual wrongful arrest cases trigger local campaigns; no national moment yet

**Status:** Ongoing. COMPAS risk assessment tool (used in 46 US states) challenged in Wisconsin Supreme Court (State v. Loomis, 2016) — court ruled using it doesn't violate due process despite black-box nature. ProPublica's "Machine Bias" investigation (2016) revealed COMPAS falsely flagged Black defendants as high-risk at nearly 2x the rate of white defendants. Multiple wrongful arrests due to facial recognition: Robert Williams (Detroit), Michael Oliver (New Orleans), Alonzo Sawyer — all Black men misidentified by algorithm.

Organizations Fighting This:
- **Algorithmic Justice League** (Joy Buolamwini): https://www.ajl.org — art + research hybrid, very close to Benjamin's model
- **Brennan Center for Justice**: https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/predictive-policing-explained
- **Pretrial Justice Institute**: https://www.pretrial.org
- **AI Now Institute**: https://ainowinstitute.org
- **EFF**: https://www.eff.org/issues/ai

**Funders:** MacArthur Foundation, Soros/Open Society Foundations, Ford Foundation tech/justice programs

**Connections:** Black Lives Matter, criminal justice reform, immigration (ICE deportation algorithms), democracy (voter suppression via algorithmic profiling)

**Benjamin's Opportunity:**
Joy Buolamwini (AJL founder) already does art+advocacy — Benjamin should *collaborate* rather than duplicate. The visual gap: **a judge handing down a sentence while a digital algorithm displays its "reasoning" — completely opaque — on a screen behind them**. Or: a physical scale where one side holds a person and the other holds a dataset. The person always loses because the dataset weighs more. No one has made the *weight* of algorithmic evidence tangible.

**Upcoming trigger:** Any new high-profile wrongful arrest case; potential Supreme Court case challenging COMPAS; Illinois pretrial reform legislation (2023 Illinois Pretrial Fairness Act is being challenged and watched nationally).

## 8. AI Environmental Cost

- Narrative: 4/5 — "Your chatbot query uses X gallons of water" — visceral and shareable

- Permission: 3/5 — "AI will solve climate change" counter-narrative is powerful
- Trigger: 2/5 — Local community organizing against data centers is the early organizing

**Status:** IEA projects data center electricity demand doubling by 2026 (from ~200TWh to ~400TWh globally). Microsoft, Google, Amazon have signed nuclear deals (Microsoft + Three Mile Island, Google + Kairos Power, Amazon + multiple) to power AI data centers — feeding nuclear resurgence debate. Water consumption: training a single large language model uses approximately 700,000 liters of freshwater. Google's water use increased 20% year-over-year (2023) due to AI. Local communities fighting data center siting in drought-prone areas (Arizona, Texas, Chile). AI Now Institute 2025 report notes federal government already "bailing out" AI industry with regulatory changes and public subsidies, protecting companies from market correction.

**Funders:** Climate funders (Bloomberg Philanthropies, Bezos Earth Fund — ironic given AWS) beginning to fund AI-climate research; MacArthur Foundation
