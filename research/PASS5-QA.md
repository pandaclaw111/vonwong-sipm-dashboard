## 5. WHAT'S STILL MISSING

### If this research were 20% better, what would be added?

**1. Direct quotes from movement participants**
- The research synthesizes scholarly and journalistic sources but lacks first-person accounts
- Adding 3-5 quotes from actual organizers would ground the analysis

**2. Failure case analysis for visual interventions**
- The research covers when visual interventions succeed
- Missing: cases where images backfired or had no effect (beyond brief Kevin Carter mention)
- Example gap: Has an environmental art installation ever been dismissed or co-opted? What happened?

**3. Funder relationship mapping**
- The research identifies aligned funders (Ford, OSF, Omidyar)
- Missing: Who at these foundations is the right contact? What have they funded recently that's comparable?

**4. Global South partnership development**
- The research emphasizes Global South perspectives
- Missing: Specific organizations or individuals Benjamin could partner with
- Example: Who is the Pacific Climate Warriors contact? Who leads Kenya Gen Z organizing?

**5. Legal/liability considerations**
- Some of the proposed images (especially "Democracy's Algorithm" with facial recognition critique) could attract legal attention
- Missing: Any analysis of defamation, IP, or security risks for the proposed work

**6. Production logistics**
- The 5 visual concepts are ideas, not production plans
- Missing: Budget ranges, timeline estimates, venue partnerships, distribution strategy

**7. Measurement framework**
- How will Benjamin know if the intervention "worked"?
- Missing: Metrics for success beyond intuition (media coverage? policy change? movement scores?)

**8. Counter-movement analysis**
- Every movement has opposition
- Missing: How will opponents (fossil fuel industry, surveillance companies, far-right parties) respond to Benjamin's work?
- What counter-narratives should be anticipated?
