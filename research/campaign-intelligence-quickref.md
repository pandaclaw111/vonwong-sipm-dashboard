## CAMPAIGN BRIEF STRUCTURE

1. **Core Tension** — One sentence. The injustice.
2. **SIPM Score** — XX/25 + recommendation.
3. **The Villain** — Specific company. Harm + claim + gap + fear + revenue.
4. **The Victim** — Named person/community. Location + what's happening + the number.
5. **The Opportunity** — Window + win/loss stakes + deadline.
6. **3 Narrative Frames** — Each with: target audience, emotion, 3 slogans, visual language.
7. **3 Installations** — Each with: concept, scale, location, emotional arc, logistics 1-10, the reveal.
8. **Actors** — Who to engage, who to target.
9. **Why Now** — The inflection point.
10. **Actions** — 30 days / 1-3 months / 3-12 months.
11. **Open Questions** — What needs human research.
12. **Confidence Levels** — What's solid vs. needs verification.

## THE SIPM: 5 CONDITIONS FOR TIPPING

| Condition | Low (1) | High (5) |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Grievance Saturation | Niche awareness | Visceral, personal, growing fast |
| Narrative Availability | No clear story | Single story with specific villain |
| Org Infrastructure | Scattered individuals | Dense network, ready to activate |
| Permission Structure | Culturally hostile | Politically and culturally viable |
| Trigger Potential | No plausible triggers | Specific dateable upcoming event |

**Key insight:** The trigger doesn't create the conditions. The conditions determine when the trigger ignites. Build conditions relentlessly; be ready to amplify when the trigger comes.

## TRIGGER TYPES THAT WORK (From 100-Case Pattern Library)

| Type | Example | Why It Works |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Undeniable video | George Floyd (BLM 2020) | Can't be rationalized away |
| Visceral single image | Turtle + straw (plastics) | Bridges abstract harm to everyday object |
| Bridge object | Straw in turtle / Rana Plaza label in rubble | Familiar + horrifying = personal accountability |
| Participatory hashtag | #MeToo / #EndSARS | Converts spectators to participants |
| Institutional moment | Shein IPO / Malawi election annulled | Specific, dateable, financially/legally material |
| Product unavailability | Wikipedia blackout | Abstract threat becomes immediate |
| Historical visual echo | Standing Rock dogs ← civil rights era | Activates memory of prior injustice |
| Martyrdom / self-sacrifice | Bouazizi / Yeb Saño fasting | Embodies millions in one specific human |
| Peaceful defender attacked | Maathai beaten planting trees | Contradiction (violence against peaceful act) is the image |
| Near-miss galvanizer | Argentina 2018 green wave near-miss | Proves possibility while maintaining urgency |
| Media erasure exposed | Nakate cropped from photo | The absence becomes the campaign |

**Note:** Statistical reports almost never work as triggers. Triggers need to be visceral, specific, impossible to look away from, and — ideally — bridge between abstract harm and familiar everyday object.

## WHEN ART MOVES THE NEEDLE VS. BACKGROUND NOISE

**Art moves the needle when:**
- Issue has high grievance but no emotional face (Bridge Object function)
- Organizational infrastructure ready to convert emotional activation to action
- The image arrives BEFORE the trigger (pre-positioning for the wave)
- The campaign creates a replicable visual language for local campaigns globally
- Art documents the gap between claims and reality (legal evidence function)

**Art is background noise when:**
- No organizational infrastructure to convert viewing into action
- Decision-maker is non-accountable (appointed regulators, authoritarian governments)
- Opponent's pre-response already deployed (recycling narrative, greenwashing defense)
- The tip has already happened and art arrives late
- Images are of "the issue" generally rather than a SPECIFIC place being destroyed RIGHT NOW

## POLYCRISIS MOVEMENT RULES

1. **No issue is isolated.** Fast fashion = plastics + petrochemicals + sacrifice zones + labor colonialism. Connect them.

2. **Polycrisis accelerates tipping** when crises share a common villain. Fragments movements when crises imply competing solutions.

3. **The highest-leverage campaigns** connect 2-3 crisis narratives under one structural cause. That's a polycrisis campaign.

4. **25% threshold (Centola)**: A committed minority needs ~25% saturation in a social network before broader norms tip. The first 20% of movement-building feels like nothing is happening. It isn't. You're building the precondition.

5. **Images you make today** might be the trigger for a tipping point in 2027. The work compounds. Never wasted.
