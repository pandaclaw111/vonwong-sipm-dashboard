### A WEF Global Risks Report–Style Risk Interconnection Analysis for Social Movements
*Research compiled for Benjamin Von Wong / SIPM Project — March 2026*

## Preface: The Analytical Framework

The World Economic Forum's Global Risks Report uses a **polycrisis model**: risks are not isolated events but nodes in an interconnected network. When one node tips, it accelerates others. The 2025 edition identified misinformation/disinformation, extreme weather, state conflict, AI-generated harm, and erosion of state sovereignty as the top 5 short-term risks by likelihood — and crucially, mapped the **cascade pathways between them**.

This document applies that exact methodology to the 20 social movements in the SIPM universe. Each movement is a **risk node**. Each connection is a **cascade pathway**. The goal: find the compound activation points where Benjamin's visual practice can land at maximum leverage.

**The 5 WEF Categories applied to SIPM movements:**
- 🔴 **Economic** — extraction economy, inequality, debt, labor displacement
- 🟢 **Environmental** — extraction, pollution, climate loss & damage
- 🟠 **Geopolitical** — democratic erosion, authoritarianism, state surveillance
- 🟡 **Societal** — invisible harm, health, displacement, gig labor
- 🔵 **Technological** — AI power, surveillance, digital rights, platform harms

---

## THE META-NARRATIVE

> **"The same logic that poisons your body poisons your democracy."**

All 20 movements share a single structural root: **the externalization of cost**. The extraction economy externalizes environmental cost. Authoritarian states externalize the cost of control onto bodies and communities. Corporations externalize labor costs through algorithms. Tech companies externalize the cost of AI onto water, energy, and ghost workers. PFAS manufacturers externalize the cost of profit onto human blood.

The meta-frame is: **Who pays for the cheap thing?** The answer is always the same: the most vulnerable person, in the most vulnerable geography, at the farthest remove from the decision-maker.

This is the WEF's "polycrisis" given a human body. The externalized costs accumulate in the same places — the DRC mine, the Atacama desert, the migrant worker's app, the child's microplastic-laden blood. **The villain isn't a company or a politician. The villain is the structural permission to make other people pay for your profit.**

Secondary meta-frame: **Legibility vs. Invisibility**. The movements that struggle most — PFAS, algorithmic health discrimination, AI ghost workers, microplastics — share the problem that their harm is invisible or technically complex. Benjamin's visual practice is precisely the technology for converting invisible structural harm into legible public outrage.

**Within-Category Connections:**
- ECO-1 → ECO-2 → ECO-3: The **extraction chain**. Fast fashion drives demand for cheap synthetic fibers and batteries; cobalt mines supply EV/electronics; deep sea mining is next-frontier extraction for the same minerals. One supply chain, three catastrophes.

- ECO-6 → ECO-7 → ECO-8: The **labor displacement cascade**. AI displaces formal workers (ECO-6); displaces them into gig platforms managed by algorithms (ECO-7); the AI itself depends on invisible Global South labor (ECO-8) — a closed loop of value extraction.
- ECO-5 ↔ ECO-8: Both are debates about *who gets paid for work already done* — climate reparations for extracted carbon, payment for extracted data/labor.

**Cascade trigger:** If AI labor displacement accelerates faster than social safety nets adapt (likely 2026–2028 as LLM job impacts become measurable), ECO-6 will cascade into mass social disruption, feeding Cluster GEO and SOC nodes.

## SECTION 2: THE RISK INTERCONNECTION MAP

### Primary Cross-Category Cascades

The following represent the highest-impact risk pathways — where a trigger in one category cascades across three or more others.

---

**CASCADE 1: "The Extraction Chain"**
```
ECO-1 (Fast Fashion) 
  → ENV-1 (Atacama sacrifice zone)
  → ENV-4 (Microplastics) [synthetic fibers = primary ocean microplastic source]
  → SOC-1 (Body contamination)
  → SOC-3 (Healthcare denials) [contamination-linked illness denied coverage]
  → ECO-5 (Loss & Damage) [who pays for poisoned communities?]
```
**Compound image opportunity:** The garment that travels from a factory → to your skin → to a dump → to the ocean → to a blood test → to a health insurance denial. One object, six crises.

---

**CASCADE 2: "The AI Power Loop"**
```
TECH-8 (AI displaces workers)
  → ECO-8 (Displaced workers become ghost workers)
  → TECH-5 (Digital colonialism — Global South data extraction)
  → ECO-6 (Value captured by AI companies, not workers)
  → GEO-5 (AI enables surveillance of displaced/migrant populations)
  → GEO-1 (Anti-authoritarian backlash)
  → TECH-2 (Regulatory pressure: EU AI Act)
```
**The loop:** AI displaces workers → displaces them into the informal economy that feeds AI → AI is then used to surveill and control those same displaced populations → sparking the resistance that produces regulation → but regulation lags the deployment by 5+ years.

---

**CASCADE 3: "The Body Is the Sacrifice Zone"**
```
ENV-5 (PFAS industrial discharge)
  → SOC-2 (PFAS in human blood)
  → ENV-4 (Microplastics in human blood)
  → SOC-1 (Compound body burden)
  → SOC-3 (Algorithmic insurance denial for contamination-linked illness)
  → ECO-5 (Who pays? Loss & Damage logic applied domestically)
```
**The cascade:** Industrial contamination accumulates in human bodies; those bodies then face AI-driven denial of healthcare they need because of the contamination; the same logic as international climate loss & damage — the people who caused the harm refuse to pay for it.

---

**CASCADE 4: "The Democracy Erosion Machine"**
```
TECH-1 (AI surveillance infrastructure built)
  → GEO-5 (ICE biometrics / immigration enforcement)
  → GEO-1 (US anti-authoritarian mobilization)
  → GEO-2 (France far-right normalizes similar surveillance)
  → TECH-5 (Surveillance tech exported to Global South)
  → GEO-7 (Digital colonialism: authoritarian infrastructure export)
```
**The cascade:** Surveillance tools normalized for immigration enforcement in democracies get exported to authoritarian states, completing a loop where democratic erosion enables authoritarian consolidation globally.

---

**CASCADE 5: "The Green Tech Contradiction"**
```
TECH-6 (AI boom drives massive energy/water demand)
  → ENV-6 (Data center footprint grows)
  → ECO-3 (Demand for rare earth minerals spikes)
  → ENV-3 (Deep sea mining justified by "green tech" needs)
  → ECO-2 (Cobalt mining DRC intensifies)
  → ENV-2 (Ecological destruction in DRC)
  → ECO-5 (Loss & Damage — DRC never compensated)
```
**The cascade:** The AI "revolution" and the "clean energy transition" both require massive mineral extraction — from the DRC, from the ocean floor, from sacrifice zones. The green future is being built on the same extraction logic as the fossil fuel past.

## SECTION 3: TOP 5 COMPOUND OPPORTUNITIES FOR BENJAMIN

### COMPOUND OPPORTUNITY 1: "The Forever Body"
**Movements Combined:** Microplastics (ENV-4/SOC-1) + PFAS (ENV-5/SOC-2) + Algorithmic Health Denial (SOC-3)

**The Shared Story:** Industrial corporations have deposited chemicals into human bodies that will never leave. Then, when those bodies get sick, an algorithm denies their insurance claim. The harm is invisible; the denial is automated; the responsibility is diffused across the supply chain.

**Shared Villain:** The chemical-industrial complex (3M, Chemours, DuPont for PFAS; synthetic textile industry for microplastics) + UnitedHealth for the denial layer.

**Shared Community:** Anyone with blood (literally — PFAS is detectable in 97% of Americans). Strongest organizing community: rural communities near military bases (PFAS contamination hotspots) and inland communities near textile/synthetic manufacturing.

**Specific Window:** 2026 is a legislative inflection point for PFAS regulation — EPA rules finalized in 2024 are being litigated. UnitedHealth Congressional hearings ongoing after the Luigi Mangione moment. The simultaneous pressure creates a compound moment.

**Benjamin's Visual Role:** A medical illustration-style image: a human silhouette filled with a cross-section of their contamination load (PFAS molecules, microplastic fibers, industrial chemicals) — paired with a rejection notice from an insurance algorithm. The body as contested terrain between industrial production and healthcare denial. **Unprecedented image in activism — nobody has made this.**

**Historical Precedent:** Love Canal (1978) + toxic tort litigation created the template. But that was one location, one chemical. The "forever body" story is everywhere simultaneously — 8 billion people are contaminated. The scale is new.

### COMPOUND OPPORTUNITY 2: "The Invisible Worker Stack"
**Movements Combined:** AI Ghost Workers (ECO-8/SOC-4) + Gig Algorithmic Management (ECO-7/SOC-5) + AI Labor Displacement (ECO-6/TECH-8) + Digital Colonialism (TECH-5/GEO-7)

**The Shared Story:** The AI economy runs on three tiers of invisible labor. At the top, algorithms displace visible formal workers. In the middle, gig platforms use algorithms to manage and extract value from precarious workers. At the bottom, Global South ghost workers label data and moderate content that makes the AI work — for $2/hour, no benefits, with severe psychological harm. The three tiers are never shown together.

**Shared Villain:** Amazon (warehouse surveillance + Mechanical Turk), Google/Meta/OpenAI (ghost labor dependency), Uber/Lyft/DoorDash (algorithmic gig management), all backed by the same venture capital infrastructure.

**Shared Community:** 55 million US gig workers; 500,000+ content moderators in the Philippines, Kenya, India; the estimated 30%+ of white-collar workers who will face AI displacement by 2028.

**Specific Window:** 2026–2027: The first major AI displacement studies with real employment data will be published. This will collide with ongoing gig worker organizing (NLRB cases in the US, EU Platform Work Directive implementation) and growing documentation of ghost worker conditions (TIME, The Guardian investigations already running).

**Benjamin's Visual Role:** A literal stack — a single image showing all three layers simultaneously. Perhaps: a physical data center server tower, but each rack contains a human body at work. Or: three different colored workers (formal, gig, ghost) connected by the same glowing algorithm line, all feeding the same corporate revenue chart. **The point is to make visible what the AI economy is actually built on.**

**Historical Precedent:** Lewis Hine's child labor photographs (1908–1918) — made the invisible supply chain of industrial capitalism legible to middle-class consumers. The photography itself was the advocacy technology. This is the direct lineage for Benjamin's practice.

### COMPOUND OPPORTUNITY 3: "The Green Extraction Paradox"
**Movements Combined:** Deep Sea Mining (ECO-3/ENV-3) + Cobalt Mining DRC (ECO-2/ENV-2) + AI Environmental Cost (ENV-6/TECH-6) + Fast Fashion Atacama (ECO-1/ENV-1)

**The Shared Story:** Every "sustainable" technology — electric vehicles, AI servers, renewable energy — requires extraction from sacrifice zones. The geography of sacrifice is consistent: it is always the Global South, always the most biodiverse ecosystems, always communities with the least political power. The "green transition" is being built on the same colonial extraction logic as the fossil fuel economy.

**Shared Villain:** Tech companies (Apple, Google, Tesla, Microsoft) who brand themselves as climate leaders while their supply chains depend on DRC cobalt, ocean floor mining, and Atacama water depletion. The fossil fuel industry, which supports deep sea mining as a distraction from subsidy reform.

**Shared Community:** DRC mining communities, Chilean Indigenous communities (Atacama), Pacific Island nations opposing deep sea mining, environmental scientists, tech workers increasingly uncomfortable with their industry's material footprint.

**Specific Window:** 2026: The International Seabed Authority (ISA) is under pressure to finalize deep sea mining regulations — the most concrete near-term decision point. The ISA meeting in 2026 will either open or delay commercial deep sea mining for a decade. Simultaneously, the Global Plastics Treaty negotiations continue, and COP31 (2026, Brazil) will feature the Green Transition vs. Extraction debate prominently.

**Benjamin's Visual Role:** A split-world image: above the water, gleaming tech offices and EV charging stations. Below, the ocean floor stripped by mining robots. Or: a single EV battery, opened up to show the geography of its components — the DRC mine, the Chilean desert, the ocean floor — in one image. **The "clean economy" made materially honest.**

**Historical Precedent:** The campaign against blood diamonds (1998–2003) that led to the Kimberley Process — made a luxury consumer product's supply chain legible enough to create political pressure for regulation. The same logic, applied to the green economy.

### COMPOUND OPPORTUNITY 4: "Democracy's Algorithm"
**Movements Combined:** AI Surveillance/ICE Biometrics (GEO-5/TECH-1) + US Anti-Authoritarianism (GEO-1) + Digital Colonialism (GEO-7/TECH-5) + EU AI Act (GEO-6/TECH-2) + Deepfakes/Creator Rights (TECH-3)

**The Shared Story:** A new infrastructure of authoritarian control is being built inside democracies, using tools developed in democracies, and exported to authoritarian states. The AI that identifies your face at a protest can also identify you at a border, in a refugee camp, in a voting line. The deepfake that destroys a creator's career can also fabricate a political opponent's crimes. These are not separate concerns — they are the same technology deployed across different domains of social control.

**Shared Villain:** The surveillance technology industry (Palantir, Clearview AI, Amazon Rekognition, DataMinr) + governments that normalize each application step by step + social media platforms that provide the training data.

**Shared Community:** Immigrants and asylum seekers (ICE biometrics); journalists and dissidents (surveillance export); artists and creators (deepfakes); teenagers (platform mental health); activists (protest tracking). An unusually broad coalition.

**Specific Window:** 2026: EU AI Act high-risk AI provisions come into force. US 2026 midterms will feature AI-generated political content at unprecedented scale. ICE deportation cases will generate legal challenges that surface biometric surveillance evidence in court for the first time.

**Benjamin's Visual Role:** A single human face, fractured into the different databases that contain it — ICE, social media, healthcare, financial, electoral. Or: a protest crowd photographed from above, with each face tagged by a different corporate/state actor simultaneously. **The face as contested territory.**

**Historical Precedent:** Orwell's *1984* gave a cultural frame, but the visual tradition here is sparse. The most effective recent predecessor is Ai Weiwei's work on surveillance and state power — but it focused on China. The US/EU version of this story hasn't been told visually at scale.

### COMPOUND OPPORTUNITY 5: "The Loss & Damage Matrix"
**Movements Combined:** Loss & Damage/Climate Finance (ECO-5/ENV-8) + Fossil Fuel Subsidies (ECO-4/ENV-7) + DRC Cobalt (ECO-2) + Atacama (ECO-1) + AI Ghost Workers (ECO-8) + Digital Colonialism (TECH-5)

**The Shared Story:** The Global South is owed money — for extracted carbon, extracted minerals, extracted labor, extracted data. The Loss & Damage framework established at COP27 and COP28 created a legal/moral language for climate reparations. The same logic applies with equal force to mining reparations, data labor reparations, and digital colonialism reparations. The "loss and damage" concept is a *general theory of extraction debt* — not just a climate finance mechanism.

**Shared Villain:** G7 governments (US, EU, UK, Canada, Japan) that simultaneously receive extraction value AND resist paying for extraction harm. The World Bank and IMF, whose structural adjustment programs created the conditions for extraction dependency.

**Shared Community:** Climate-frontline communities in Bangladesh, Pakistan, Mozambique; DRC mining communities; Kenyan content moderators; Filipino ghost workers; Chilean Indigenous communities. A Global South solidarity coalition that doesn't yet have a unified frame.

**Specific Window:** COP31 in Brazil (2026) is hosted by the Global South and will push Loss & Damage implementation harder than any previous COP. It coincides with the 5th anniversary of the COP26 Glasgow commitments — a built-in accountability moment. The Brazilian presidency will explicitly connect climate debt to broader extraction debt.

**Benjamin's Visual Role:** A global map where every extraction site (mines, oil fields, data centers, garment factories) is connected by glowing lines to the government that benefits from it — and the communities that paid for it. An accounting ledger as art. Or: a single person representing each form of extraction debt they carry — climate, mineral, digital, labor — stacked in their body. **Making the invisible global economy legible in one image.**

**Historical Precedent:** Jubilee 2000's campaign for debt cancellation (1996–2000) — collected 24 million signatures, led to $100B in debt relief, by making "the debt" a simple moral narrative. The Loss & Damage moment is structurally analogous — a new moral accounting framework that needs a visual language.

### CASCADE 1: "The Extraction Chain" — Evidence

**Fast Fashion → Microplastics → Body Contamination**

- **Source**: [Wikipedia: Microplastics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microplastics); [AFP Atacama Investigation](https://www.theguardian.com/global-development/2021/nov/08/the-fashion-graveyard-the-chilean-desert-overwhelmed-by-fast-fashion-waste)

- **Source**: [Wikipedia: UnitedHealth Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UnitedHealth_Group)

- **Surveillance of displaced**: ICE's use of Palantir and Clearview AI to track undocumented immigrants is documented; these tools draw on datasets that include gig worker location data.

## SECTION 10: WEF METHODOLOGY — EXPLICITLY STATED (Pass 3 Additions)

*Clarifying what the WEF Global Risks Report methodology actually does and doesn't do*

### What the WEF Methodology Actually Is

The WEF Global Risks Report uses several analytical methods:

**1. Expert Survey (Delphi Method)**
- ~800 global experts from WEF's Global Risks Advisory Board complete a structured questionnaire
- Each risk rated on two scales: LIKELIHOOD (1-5) and IMPACT (1-5)
- Results aggregated to produce risk rankings

**2. Risk Interconnection Mapping**
- Experts asked to identify which risks they believe are directly connected
- Connections drawn if >X% of experts agree on the link
- Produces a network graph visualization

**3. Five Risk Categories**
- Economic, Environmental, Geopolitical, Societal, Technological
- Categories are WEF's taxonomy, not universally agreed

**4. Quantitative Risk Assessment**
- Includes time horizons (short-term: 2 years; long-term: 10 years)
- Includes regional breakdowns
- Some probabilistic modeling

### How This Document Differs

This SIPM movement intersection analysis does NOT replicate the WEF methodology. What it does:

**Uses the WEF five-category taxonomy** — this is a borrowed structure, not a finding
**Uses the WEF "cascade pathway" concept** — risks connected by causal or amplification relationships
**Does NOT conduct expert surveys** — this is qualitative synthesis, not Delphi
**Does NOT produce likelihood/impact scores** — probabilities would require more rigorous methods

**The honest characterization**: This document applies WEF-style interconnection analysis to social movements using available evidence. It is an analytical framework, not a predictive model.

## APPENDIX: THE WEF CASCADE METHODOLOGY — APPLIED TO SIPM

The WEF uses the following criteria to determine which risk connections to draw on their interconnection map:

1. **Causal connection**: Risk A directly causes or accelerates Risk B
2. **Shared driver**: Both risks arise from the same underlying structural cause
3. **Amplification**: The presence of Risk A increases the severity/likelihood of Risk B, even without direct causation
4. **Community overlap**: The same population is exposed to both risks simultaneously
