## 1. GLOBAL SOUTH MOVEMENT SLOGANS & HASHTAGS

### Arab Spring (2010-2011)

**Egypt:** "Bread, Freedom, Social Justice" (عيش، حرية، عدالة اجتماعية / Aish, Hurriya, 'Adala Igtima'iyya)
- Origin: January 2011 Tahrir Square protests
- Synthesized three core demands of the revolution
- Became the most enduring slogan, later adopted by other Arab Spring countries
- Source: [Wikipedia: Arab Spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Spring)

**Tunisia:** "Dégage!" (Get out! / ارحل)
- Origin: December 2010, directed at President Ben Ali
- The slogan's power was in its single-word simplicity — a clear dismissal
- Became template for other Arab Spring countries
- Source: [Wikipedia: Arab Spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Spring)

**Syria:** "The people want the fall of the regime" (الشعب يريد إسقاط النظام / Al-sha'b yurid isqat al-nizam)
- Adapted from original Arab Spring slogan "The people want..."
- Demonstrates linguistic mutation across movements
- Source: [Wikipedia: Arab Spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Spring)

### Latin America

**Chile (2019-2020):** "No son 30 pesos, son 30 años" (It's not 30 pesos, it's 30 years)
- Origin: October 2019 metro fare protests in Santiago
- Referenced 30 years of neoliberal policy since Pinochet
- Triggered by a 30-peso fare increase but became rejection of entire economic model
- Led to constitutional convention and rewrite (though later referendum rejected draft)
- "Chile despertó" (Chile woke up) was another key slogan
- Source: [Wikipedia: 2019-2020 Chilean protests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019%E2%80%932020_Chilean_protests)

**Argentina (2015-present):** "Ni Una Menos" (Not One [Woman] Less)
- Origin: June 3, 2015 Buenos Aires, after murder of 14-year-old Chiara Paez
- Name derived from 1995 phrase by Mexican poet Susana Chávez: "Ni una muerta más" (Not one more dead woman) — Chávez herself was assassinated in 2011
- Mobilized 200,000 in Buenos Aires alone; spread across Latin America
- Led to creation of Registry of Femicides and Ministry for Women, Gender, and Diversity
- Credited as catalyst for Argentina's legalization of first-trimester abortion (December 2020)
- Spread to: Peru (2016, largest protest in Peruvian history), Mexico (2020 women's strike), Puerto Rico (2021)
- Source: [Wikipedia: Ni una menos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ni_una_menos)

### Africa

**South Africa (2015-2016):** #FeesMustFall
- Origin: October 12, 2015 at University of Witwatersrand
- Triggered by 10.5% fee increase announcement
- Spread to University of Cape Town, Rhodes University, then nationwide
- Resulted in: No fee increases in 2016; President Zuma announcement from Union Buildings
- R800 million in property damage; 619 arrests
- Connected to #RhodesMustFall (2015) decolonization movement
- Source: [Wikipedia: FeesMustFall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fees_Must_Fall)

**Nigeria (2020):** #EndSARS
- Targeting Special Anti-Robbery Squad (SARS) police brutality
- Led to Lekki toll gate massacre (October 20, 2020)
- Source: [Wikipedia: End SARS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/End_SARS)

**Kenya (2024):** Kenya Finance Bill protests / "Occupy Parliament"
- Gen Z-led protests against controversial tax hikes
- Part of broader global Gen Z protest wave
- Source: [Wikipedia: Gen Z protests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gen_Z_protests)

### Asia

**Hong Kong (2019-2020):** "Five Demands, Not One Less" (五大訴求，缺一不可)
- Origin: June 2019 anti-extradition bill protests
- The five demands: withdrawal of bill, retraction of "riot" classification, release of protesters, independent inquiry, universal suffrage
- "Liberate Hong Kong, revolution of our times" (光復香港，時代革命) was banned under National Security Law
- Largest protest: estimated 2 million on June 16, 2019
- Source: [Wikipedia: 2019-2020 Hong Kong protests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019%E2%80%932020_Hong_Kong_protests)

**Sudan (2018-2019):** Sudanese Revolution slogans
- Part of the "Second Arab Spring"
- Led by Sudanese Professionals Association
- Key women's organization: MANSAM (Women of Sudanese Civic and Political Groups)
- Resulted in: Overthrow of Omar al-Bashir (April 2019), but followed by Khartoum massacre (128 killed) and eventual military coup (2021)
- First female Chief Justice: Nemat Abdullah Khair (October 2019)
- Source: [Wikipedia: 2019 Sudanese revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_Sudanese_revolution)

## 2. NON-WESTERN MOVEMENT THEORY (Asef Bayat)

### Asef Bayat's Key Concepts

**Background:**
- Iranian-American professor of sociology at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign
- Previously: American University in Cairo (17 years), director of ISIM at Leiden University
- Key works: *Life as Politics*, *Making Islam Democratic*, *Street Politics*
- Source: [Wikipedia: Asef Bayat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asef_Bayat)

**"Quiet Encroachment of the Ordinary"**
- Concept: How marginalized communities in authoritarian contexts create change WITHOUT formal organization
- Describes "the silent, patient, protracted, and pervasive advancement of ordinary people on those who are propertied and powerful in order to survive and improve their lives"
- Fundamentally challenges Western movement theory which assumes formal organization

**"Non-Movements"**
- Concept: Collective actions by non-collective actors — the shared practices of large numbers of ordinary people without deliberate coordination
- Examples: Millions individually encroaching on public space, women quietly defying dress codes, youth creating alternative lifestyles
- These "non-movements" can suddenly become active movements when triggered by an event

**"Street Politics"**
- The urban street as a site of political contestation in the Middle East
- Particularly relevant for understanding movements in informal economies

**"Post-Islamism"**
- Term coined by Bayat in 1995 essay "The Coming of a Post-Islamist Society"
- Seeks to reconcile Islamic values with democratic principles and civil rights
- Response to shortcomings of Iranian Islamic Revolution

**Key Critique of Western Theory:**
- Western social movement theories overemphasize democratic contexts
- Framework needed that captures popular agency in non-democratic settings
- Subaltern populations actively mobilize through diverse traditions — leftists, unionists, women, Islamists, post-Islamists — rather than passively enduring

## 3. ERICA CHENOWETH: UPDATED FINDINGS

**2020 Essay: "The Future of Nonviolent Resistance"**
- Published in Journal of Democracy
- Key finding: Nonviolent protest movements in the 2010s were MORE COMMON than previous decades but LESS EFFECTIVE
- Source: [Wikipedia: Erica Chenoweth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erica_Chenoweth)

**Four factors Chenoweth identified as MISSING in 2010s movements:**
1. "Careful planning, organization, training, and coalition-building PRIOR to mass mobilization" — protests occurring first, organization after
2. Momentum in "growing in size and diversity" — using techniques like strikes and civil disobedience
3. Inclusion of a NON-INTERNET component to organizing
4. "Strategies for maintaining unity and discipline" in rejecting violent tactics

**Chenoweth's optimism about recent movements:**
- COVID-19 protests were "updating and renewing the outdated playbook"
- Moving away from "relying exclusively on protest"
- "Building resilient coalitions with a greater capacity for bringing about lasting transformation"

**Nonviolent Action Lab:**
- As of January 2025, Chenoweth directs the Nonviolent Action Lab at Harvard's Ash Center

**Crowd Counting Consortium:**
- Co-founded with Jeremy Pressman in 2017
- Tracks protest participation data

## 4. "WE ARE THE 99%" — ORIGIN VERIFIED

**Origin:**
- Launched as a Tumblr blog page in late August 2011 (before September 17 OWS)
- Source: [Wikipedia: Occupy movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupy_movement) — cites Mother Jones and Foreign Policy

**Adbusters role:**
- Co-founder Kalle Lasn registered OccupyWallStreet.org on June 9, 2011

## 5. PACIFIC CLIMATE WARRIORS

**Organization:**
- Pacific Climate Warriors (also known as 350 Pacific) — part of 350.org since 2011
- Grassroots movement for climate justice from Pacific island states
- Source: [Wikipedia: Pacific Climate Warriors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Climate_Warriors)

**Core Message:**
- "We are not drowning. We are fighting."
- Reframes Pacific Islanders from victims to agents

**Notable Actions:**

**2014 Newcastle Canoe Blockade (October 17, 2014):**
- 30 members from 12 Pacific countries (Fiji, Kiribati, Papua New Guinea, Marshall Islands, Tokelau, Tonga, Tuvalu, Vanuatu)
- Paddled traditional canoes to blockade world's largest coal port
- Blocked 8 of 12 scheduled coal ships
- Canoes built in Pacific Islands using traditional methods, shipped to Australia for the action
- Demand: Fossil fuel companies take responsibility for climate damage

**COP26 London protests (2021):**
- Represented at protests before Glasgow negotiations

**Awards:**
- 2020 Pax Christi International Peace Award

**Educational work:**
- Projects in 15 Pacific countries to explain climate consequences to young people

## 8. HISTORICAL COALITION FORMATION

### Occupy Movement Pre-history (2009-2011)

**California Student Movement (2009-2010):**
- Occupied campus buildings against budget cuts, tuition hikes
- "Occupy Everything, Demand Nothing" emerged here
- Wheeler Hall occupation (UC Berkeley)

**Spanish Indignados / 15-M Movement (May 2011):**
- Camps in Madrid and across Spain
- By end of May: "hundreds of camps around Spain and across the world" (Manuel Castells)
- Some journalists date global occupy movement from here, not September 2011

**Democracy Village (2010):**
- Set up outside British Parliament in London
- Direct inspiration for Occupy

**Call for October 15 Global Protest:**
- May 30, 2011: Spanish Indignados leader called for worldwide protest
- Inspired by Arab Spring, South Korea's democratization movements (5.18 Movement 1980, June Democracy Movement 1987)

**Occupy Dataran (July 2011):**
- Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia
- SEVEN WEEKS before Occupy Wall Street
- Often overlooked as precursor

## 9. VISUAL ACTIVISM EVIDENCE

### Evidence for Visual Effectiveness

**Pacific Climate Warriors Canoe Blockade (2014):**
- Visual of traditional canoes blocking coal ships created powerful contrast
- Image shared globally, generated media coverage beyond typical protest
- Led to Pax Christi Peace Award recognition

- Adbusters poster: Dancer atop Charging Bull statue

## 12. VISUAL ART CHANGING MOVEMENT TRAJECTORIES

### Historical Precedents

**Napalm Girl (1972):**
- Nick Ut's photograph shifted American public opinion on Vietnam War
- Single image credited with accelerating withdrawal

**Tank Man (1989):**
- Tiananmen Square image became global symbol of individual resistance
- Demonstrated power of visual confrontation

**Alan Kurdi (2015):**
- Image of drowned Syrian toddler on Turkish beach
- Changed European refugee policy within days
- Germany opened borders shortly after

**George Floyd Video (2020):**
- 8 minutes 46 seconds footage sparked global protests
- Demonstrated power of citizen documentation

**Patterns:**
1. Single powerful image can shift public opinion faster than months of organizing
2. Images of vulnerability (children, peaceful protesters facing force) generate most sympathy
3. Visual evidence of injustice creates "undeniable moment"
4. Images that create emotional identification across communities most effective
